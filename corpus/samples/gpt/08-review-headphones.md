## Review: Sony WH-1000XM5 Wireless Headphones

The Sony WH-1000XM5 wireless headphones are a premium pair of over-ear headphones aimed at people who want excellent noise cancellation, strong sound quality, and long-wearing comfort. They are not the cheapest headphones on the market, but they make a strong case for themselves if you travel often, work in noisy environments, or simply want a polished listening experience.

The first thing you notice about the WH-1000XM5 is the design. Compared with many older over-ear headphones, they look cleaner and more modern. The headband is slim, the ear cups are smooth, and the overall appearance is understated rather than flashy. They feel lightweight in the hand, and that continues when you put them on. The padding on the ear cups is soft and comfortable, and the clamping force is gentle enough for long listening sessions. I was able to wear them for several hours without feeling much pressure around my ears or on the top of my head.

That said, the design does come with one drawback: the headphones do not fold inward like some previous models. The ear cups rotate flat, but the overall size of the carrying case is still fairly large. If you travel with a small backpack or want something ultra-compact, this may be slightly inconvenient. The case itself is well made, though, and it offers good protection.

Sound quality is one of the strongest parts of these headphones. The WH-1000XM5 delivers a warm, full sound with plenty of bass, clear vocals, and smooth treble. Pop, hip-hop, electronic music, and podcasts all sound excellent. The bass is strong but usually controlled, so it does not overwhelm the rest of the mix. Vocals come through clearly, which makes these headphones good not only for music but also for audiobooks, videos, and calls.

If you prefer a very neutral, studio-style sound, you may find the default tuning a little bass-heavy. Fortunately, Sony’s companion app includes an equalizer, so you can adjust the sound to your liking. This is a major advantage because it allows the headphones to suit a wide range of preferences. With a few small changes in the app, you can make them sound brighter, flatter, or even bassier.

Noise cancellation is where the WH-1000XM5 really stands out. These headphones are excellent at reducing constant low-frequency noise, such as airplane engines, train rumble, air conditioning, and traffic. They also do a good job with general office noise, including distant conversations and keyboard sounds. They do not completely erase every sudden sound, but they create a much quieter environment and make it easier to focus.

The transparency mode is also impressive. With ambient sound turned on, you can hear your surroundings without taking the headphones off. This is useful when walking outside, listening for announcements, or having a quick conversation. Sony also includes a “speak-to-chat” feature, which automatically pauses your music when you start talking. It is clever, although it can sometimes activate when you cough, hum, or sing along, so some users may prefer to turn it off.

Battery life is another strong point. The headphones offer up to around 30 hours of listening with noise cancellation turned on, which is more than enough for several days of normal use or a long international flight. Charging is quick, and a short charge can provide several hours of playback. This makes them practical for daily use, especially if you sometimes forget to charge your devices overnight.

The controls are simple and responsive. The right ear cup uses touch controls for volume, playback, track skipping, and activating voice assistants. Swiping up or down changes the volume, while tapping pauses or resumes playback. The gestures are easy to learn and usually work well, although they can be a little sensitive if you accidentally brush the ear cup. There are also physical buttons for power and noise-cancellation modes, which is a good choice because those functions benefit from a more definite control.

Call quality is very good for wireless headphones. Voices sound clear, and the microphones do a solid job of reducing background noise. In quiet rooms, calls are crisp and natural. In busy streets or windy conditions, performance drops somewhat, but it is still better than many competing headphones. For remote work, video meetings, and casual phone calls, they are dependable.

Connectivity is generally excellent. Pairing is quick, and multipoint Bluetooth support lets you connect to two devices at the same time, such as a laptop and phone. This is especially convenient if you listen to music on your computer but want to answer calls from your phone. The connection remains stable in normal use, with minimal dropouts.

Overall, the Sony WH-1000XM5 is an outstanding pair of wireless headphones. They combine excellent noise cancellation, comfortable design, strong battery life, and enjoyable sound in a premium package. They are expensive, and the non-folding design may bother frequent travelers who need maximum portability. However, for most people looking for high-quality wireless over-ear headphones, they are easy to recommend.

**Rating: 4.7 out of 5**
