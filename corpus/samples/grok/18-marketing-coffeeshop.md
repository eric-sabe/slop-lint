**Welcome to Oakwood Coffee**

At the corner of Oak and 5th, Oakwood Coffee has been the neighborhood’s living room since 2012. We’re an independent shop run by people who live two blocks away, roast in small batches every Tuesday, and still remember how you like your cappuccino. Step inside and you’ll smell freshly ground Ethiopian and hear the low hum of neighbors catching up, students highlighting textbooks, and the occasional golden retriever waiting patiently by the door.

**Our Story**

We opened because the block needed more than another chain with identical chairs and corporate playlists. Founders Maya and Theo wanted a place where the baristas knew your dog’s name and the pastries came from the bakery across the street. Eight years later we still buy our beans directly from two farms in Guatemala and one cooperative in Kenya. We roast on a 12-kilo machine in the back room, cup every batch ourselves, and adjust the profile the moment the season changes. Nothing is automated; every shot is pulled by hand.

**What’s in the Cup**

Our espresso blend is bright with notes of stone fruit and cocoa; it pulls sweet and finishes clean. The single-origin rotation changes weekly—right now we’re featuring a washed Burundi that tastes like black tea, orange zest, and brown sugar. Milk drinks use a local dairy that delivers glass bottles twice a week; oat and almond options come from small producers in the Pacific Northwest. 

The pastry case is stocked daily by three local bakers: cardamom morning buns on Monday, chocolate-chip cookies the size of your palm on Wednesday, and a rotating savory galette on weekends. We also keep a small fridge of house-made grab-and-go items—overnight oats, egg bites, and a turmeric-ginger wellness shot that regulars swear by during cold season.

**The Space**

Sunlight pours through the south-facing windows onto reclaimed fir tables. Power outlets are plentiful but never dominate the room; we’d rather you stay and talk than just charge your laptop. A low bookshelf holds neighborhood cookbooks and children’s titles; feel free to borrow or leave one. The back corner has two armchairs and a small table that somehow always ends up hosting an impromptu chess game.

**Community First**

We keep the doors open late on the first Friday of every month for poetry night and once a month for a neighborhood cleanup followed by free drip coffee. Local artists hang work on the walls for two-month rotations; 100 % of sales go to the artist. During the holidays we collect warm coats and non-perishables for the food pantry two blocks away. None of this is marketing—it’s just what neighbors do.

**Come Say Hi**

We’re open every day from 6:30 a.m. to 6:00 p.m. (7:00 on weekends). Street parking is free after 6 and plentiful on side streets. Bike racks sit right out front, and the bus stop is at the corner. If you’re new, tell the barista—it’s on the house. If you’re a regular, we already know.

Oakwood Coffee isn’t trying to be everything to everyone. It’s trying to be exactly what this neighborhood needs: a reliable cup, a warm seat, and the quiet certainty that you belong here.
