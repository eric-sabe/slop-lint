# Sony WH-1000XM5 Wireless Headphones: A Review

When it comes to premium wireless headphones, Sony's WH-1000XM5 has established itself as a benchmark against which competitors are measured. After several weeks of daily use—across commutes, work-from-home sessions, flights, and quiet evenings at home—I've formed a comprehensive picture of what these headphones offer and where they fall short.

## Design and Comfort

The first thing you notice when picking up the XM5 is the redesign Sony implemented compared to its predecessors. Gone are the foldable hinges of the XM4, replaced by a sleeker, more streamlined silhouette with slimmer headband construction. While some longtime fans lament the loss of the folding mechanism—it makes the carrying case noticeably larger—the new design feels more premium and modern.

Comfort is where these headphones genuinely excel. The synthetic leather ear cushions are plush without being overly warm, and the lightweight build (around 250 grams) means I can wear them for hours without the fatigue that plagues bulkier models. The clamping force strikes a nice balance, staying secure during movement without squeezing your head uncomfortably. For anyone who wears glasses, the soft padding accommodates frames reasonably well, though extended sessions can still create mild pressure.

## Sound Quality

This is the heart of any headphone review, and the XM5 delivers impressively. The 30mm carbon fiber drivers produce a warm, detailed soundstage that suits a wide variety of genres. Bass is deep and punchy without overwhelming the mids, making everything from hip-hop to orchestral pieces sound rich and engaging.

The default tuning leans slightly toward a consumer-friendly profile with emphasis on low end, but Sony's companion app allows extensive equalizer customization for those who prefer a flatter or more analytical sound. High frequencies are crisp and clear, though extremely treble-sensitive listeners may occasionally find certain tracks a touch sharp. Vocals, in particular, come through with excellent clarity and presence.

Audiophiles seeking the absolute pinnacle of fidelity might lean toward wired alternatives, but for wireless listening, the XM5 sits comfortably near the top of its class. Support for LDAC codec ensures higher-quality streaming for compatible devices.

## Noise Cancellation

If there's a single category where these headphones dominate, it's active noise cancellation. Sony's dual-processor system with eight microphones produces genuinely class-leading results. The droning hum of an airplane cabin essentially disappears, and the rumble of public transit fades into the background. Even higher-frequency sounds—typically harder to cancel—are noticeably reduced.

The ambient sound mode is equally well-executed, allowing you to hear announcements or conversations without removing the headphones. The "Speak-to-Chat" feature, which automatically pauses your audio when you start talking, is clever, though I occasionally found it triggered by humming along to music.

## Battery Life and Connectivity

Sony rates the XM5 at roughly 30 hours of playback with noise cancellation enabled, and in my experience, this estimate holds up well. A quick three-minute charge yields about three hours of listening, which is a lifesaver when you're rushing out the door. The switch to USB-C charging is a welcome modernization.

Bluetooth connectivity is stable and supports multipoint pairing, allowing you to connect to two devices simultaneously—seamlessly switching between a laptop and phone, for instance. The connection rarely dropped during my testing, even in crowded environments with significant wireless interference.

## Call Quality and Smart Features

Call quality is solid thanks to the array of microphones and AI-based noise reduction. Callers reported that my voice came through clearly, even in moderately noisy settings. The touch controls on the right ear cup are responsive and intuitive, handling playback, volume, and call management with a few taps and swipes.

## Verdict

At a premium price point of around $400, the Sony WH-1000XM5 isn't an impulse purchase. However, the combination of best-in-class noise cancellation, excellent sound quality, all-day comfort, and reliable battery life justifies the investment for frequent travelers, remote workers, and anyone who values immersive audio.

The lack of a folding design and the higher price compared to the still-excellent XM4 are worth considering. But if you want one of the finest wireless headphones available today, the XM5 earns a confident recommendation.

**Rating: 4.5 / 5**
